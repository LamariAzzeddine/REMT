add a cronjob:
must be ran as sudo
(crontab -u {user} -l ; echo {job}) | crontab -u {user} -

Removes a job:
crontab -u manager1 -l | grep -v 'perl /home/mobman/test.pl'  | crontab -u manager1 -

leaves only the command/path 
echo "*/5 * * * 2 perl /home/mobman/somethinf.pl" | awk '{print $NF}'

this does everything:
echo {path} | awk '{print $NF}' | (crontab -u {user} -l | grep -v "$(cat -)") | crontab -u {user} -

Removes all jobs: 
crontab -r
